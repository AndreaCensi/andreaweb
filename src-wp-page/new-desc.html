2. Event-based Neuromorphic Perception and Control

Neuromorphic sensors are a new kind of sensor with impressive capabilities: their output is a stream of events, generated every time there is a change in the local brightness perceived by a pixel.

My research is guided by the questions:

  • Algorithms: How can we obtain "zero-latency" event-based control pipelines for agile mobile robots?
  • Theory: How can we formally say that this sensor class is better than another? For what tasks? In what environments?

My approach...

Representative works: ICRA'15, ACC'15, IROS'13.

Group

My group is currently isomorphic to the empty set. (I co-advise students in Emilio's group.)

MIT students: There is funding available for MIT PhD/MS students who want to work with me. Please contact me if interested.

Non-MIT students: It is not impossible to find a way for you to come work here, however, understand that it is really competitive. But, the walls are there for you to climb them.
The best way for this to happen is 1) get in touch with local researchers working in robotics & control; 2) do good work with them; 3) tell them to contact us. Chances are we know them and will read carefully any email from them.
