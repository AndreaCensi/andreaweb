Andrea Censi

I work in the areas of systems and robotics 🤖.

I spend most of my time in Zürich 🇨🇭 and travel often to Singapore 🇸🇬 and Cambridge, MA 🇺🇸.
This is what I do:

  • 👨‍🔬 I am Oberassistent (untranslatable, approximately “Senior Researcher”) at the Institute for Dynamics, Systems, and Control in Emilio Frazzoli's group at ETH Zürich.

    Until early 2017 I was a Research Scientist and PI at the Laboratory for Information and Decision Systems at MIT. I am still supervising some projects and industrial collaborations there.

    The Frazzoli group is looking for exceptional students and postdocs in the areas of: autonomy (especially joint learning, inference and control and motion planning) mobility-on-demand, system co-design. Feel free to contact me at acensi@ethz.ch if you are interested.

  • 👨‍💼 I am the System Architect at nuTonomy.
    We make self-driving cars 🚗.

    We're hiring in all areas of robotics, both at the senior and junior level. Feel free to contact me at andrea@nutonomy.com if you'd like to talk about these opportunities.

  • 👨‍🏫 I am Chief Technology Officer of Duckietown, a robotics education and outreach effort.

    The next edition of Duckietown organized by me will be at ETHZ during Fall 2017. We are looking for partner universities to make this a shared experience. Please contact me at censi@duckie.town if you are interested in this. Please contact the team at duckietown@mit.edu for other inquiries (press etc.)

I obtained a Ph.D. in Control & Dynamical Systems from Caltech 🌴 in 2012 in Richard Murray's group.

I grew up in Italy and obtained an M.Eng. degree in control & robotics from Sapienza University.

My research

Here's what's up: The robots are coming! Please see here to check the likelihood that your job will be replaced by a machine. But don't worry too much; the thing is, we don't really know how to design complex robotic systems, despite the headlines.

I work on the Science of Embodied Autonomy. The engineering applications of my work are towards making complex autonomous robotic systems more robust, more efficient, and easier to design. More generally, I want to understand what are the principles underlying embodied intelligence, both natural and artificial.
